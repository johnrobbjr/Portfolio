About Me

I graduated and was offered a full-time job in December of 2014. I turned it down, moved to Baltimore with a shaky part-time freelance gig, and set up shop in my basement apartment.

I learned to freelance via baptism-by-fire, and survived 7 months before shipping up to Boston to begin my Master's at Bentley University’s Human Factors in Information Design program. At Bentley I’ve learned to put the “U” in “UX” through rigorous user research and design methodologies.

Sponges are my spirit animal. I will absorb whatever information comes my way: new methodologies, information about a field I’m not familiar with, or design critiques.

Fast-paced environments suit me best: to-do lists and efficient systems bring a smile to my face. I take on leadership opportunities gladly and draw upon my past leadership experiences to command those efforts. I’ve always enjoyed teaching, and my goal is to help others learn about UX one day.

Besides all that jazz, I like to make/listen to music, take photos, code a little bit, travel when I can (Cape Town and Seville are my favorites so far), and run through the city.
