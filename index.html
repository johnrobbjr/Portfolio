About Me

Why UX?

I’m interested in UX because of its intersection between creativity, problem solving, and empathy. UX allows me to think about problems in new and creative ways by keeping people at the center of products.

My Journey

During my undergraduate studies at Pace University, I competed in mobile app design competitions and took on internships at Priceline.com and Warner Music Group in NYC.

After completing my BA in Communications, I set south to Baltimore and set up shop as a freelance UXer to further develop my skills. I then shipped up to Boston to learn about research methods and early-stage design at Bentley University. After a graduate assistantship and many late nights, I completed my MS in Human Factors in Information Design.

I was hired at Google for a 6 month contract as a UX Research Assistant for Google’s news-related products, which was extended to a year.

I turned down a full-time offer from Google to take the once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to join my girlfriend in Munich (Ich spreche nur ein bisschen Deutsch jetzt, aber ich lerne!) while she continues her graduate studies at LMU.

Everything else

Besides UX, I make and listen to music (Spotify says I like “indietronica,” whatever that means), learn German, sketch, taking photos, run through the city, and travel when I can.
